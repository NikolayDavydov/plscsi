FTP Is

Four Q&A on FTP, converted to Html from the text note found at the end of the "Thursday, January 17, 2002" edition of FtpClient.java:

1) Why did we write yet another Ftp client?

For fun, for education, and to give us an easy way to copy just the different portion of a folder of folders, to the web from the Java platform of our choice.

Alternatives we rejected included:

classic command-line `ftp`

(can't put just files that are newer or more different)
(can't put/get folders of folders)
(can't echo the mailto PASS of USER anonymous,
      without also echoing our firewall's ACCT password)
(can't substitute PASV/EPSV for PORT/EPRT per rfc1579)

java.net.URL("ftp://...")

(can't put)

`javap sun.net.ftp.FtpClient`

(less well documented and distributed than java.net)
(rumoured to be imprecise in protocol)
(lacks a main method)

http://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc1350.txt (TFtp)

(tftp can't put/get folders)

http://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc913.txt (SFtp)

(fun but ...)
(not line-buffered: eol is NUL rather than CR LF)
(no provision for including NUL in path names)

3) To find rfc's relevant to Ftp client design we recommend:

http://www.rfc-editor.org/rfcsearch.html

SEARCH: file transfer protocol
Search: RFC
Search Results Link to: HTTP
Match: EntireWord
Results Per Page: All

We also recommend searching for just "ftp" as well as "file transfer protocol": we regretfully report that these two searches found many of the same but a few different rfc's for us.

Ftp goes further back in time but quickly becomes less recognisable. Before the rfc542 of 1973, the only REPLY_0YZ is 000. Before the rfc354 of July 1972, the commands are unprintable opcodes, rather than three/four char corruptions of English.
